# Hi, I’m Ege Amirak

I am currently a PhD researcher at the Huang Lab, [Hamburg Center for Translational Immunology (HCTI)](https://www.uke.de/english/hcti/index.html), [University Medical Center Hamburg-Eppendorf (UKE)](https://www.uke.de/). Within the CRC1700 Collaborative Research Centre, my work focuses on understanding how hepatic macrophage populations of distinct ontogeny differ in their spatial localization, phenotypic adaptation, and contribution to biliary inflammation and fibrotic remodeling, combining double fate mapper mouse models, flow cytometry, spatial transcriptomics, and human PSC liver tissue to map the neuro-immune niche of the portal triad.

Before joining UKE, I completed my M.Sc. in Molecular Biology and Genetics at the [Izmir Institute of Technology](https://en.iyte.edu.tr/)under the supervision of [Assist. Prof. Dr. Atakan Ekiz](https://www.atakanekiz.com/lab.html), where I worked at the intersection of tumor biology and immunology. My master's research focused on the tumor-intrinsic role of the long non-coding RNA DANCR in melanoma immunomodulation and PI3K signaling, combining transcriptomic analyses of publicly available datasets with functional perturbations — CRISPRa, CRISPRi, shRNA, and pharmacological inhibition — in melanoma cell lines. In parallel, I collaborated remotely with Assist. Prof. Wan-Lin Lo at the University of Utah, investigating how altered TCR signaling in LAT-G135D mutant mice shapes T cell development, clonal diversity, and repertoire architecture through single-cell RNA-seq and TCR repertoire analysis.

Beyond the bench, as a Task Force member of the [young European Federation of Immunological Societies (yEFIS)](https://www.yefis.org/), I contribute to both communication efforts and event organization, playing an active role in enhancing the visibility and outreach of immunology among early-career researchers. My responsibilities include developing accessible and engaging scientific content for social media, coordinating promotional strategies, and supporting broader science communication initiatives. Through these roles, I aim to bridge scientific excellence with community engagement, fostering a more inclusive and collaborative immunology network across Europe and beyond.
